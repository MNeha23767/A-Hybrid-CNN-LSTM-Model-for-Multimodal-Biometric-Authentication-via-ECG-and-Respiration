# A-Hybrid-CNN-LSTM-Model-for-Multimodal-Biometric-Authentication-via-ECG-and-Respiration
 Biometric authentication systems have mostly used physical traits like fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris patterns. But these techniques can be spoofed and attacked by the environment.


  INTRODUCTION
One of the most secure and convenient methods of verifying
the identity of the person has become biometric authentication.
The classic biometric systems rely on external physical or
behavioral characteristics, including fingerprints, iris patterns,
faces or voice. Although these techniques are good, they
may be counterfeited, copied or altered by environmental and
physical factors. This has made internal physiological signals
including electrocardiogram (ECG) and respiration signals to
be investigated. These cues are strongly associated with the
heart and breathing system of an individual, hence they are
quite difficult to imitate.
The ECG signal is a record of the electrical activity of the
heart and it displays distinctive patterns based on anatom-
ical and physiological variations in individuals. Similarly,
breathing patterns are different because of lung capacity,
breathing rhythm and autonomic nervous system reaction,
which is another dimension of uniqueness.In our study, we
have suggested a combined deep learning-based biometric
authentication system that is applicable with the ECG and
respira-tion signals to accurately identify the user. The model
uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to obtain spatial
and morphological data of the biosignals. Then it applies
a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network to mineralize
temporal dependencies as well as dynamics with time.
The data utilized in this research is that of multi-person
physio- logical recordings of ECG and respiration data. Some
of the preprocessing methods that we use include bandpass
filtering, normalization, and segmentation to minimize noise,
balance signal scales, and extract meaningful segments to train
the model. The system is highly accurate in the classification
of people of their physiological patterns, which indicates the
ECG and respiration as promising biometric identifiers.
This is meant to assist in developing safe, contact based and
physiology-based biometric authentication systems. It provides
enhanced resistance to attacks of spoofing and preconditions
the development of real-time hardware use in the future
